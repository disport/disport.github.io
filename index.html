DMARC Organizational Domains Tracking Framework

DMARC policy is set for each sending domain by delegating to one of several centralized records, via CNAME.

organization.com is permitted to accept DMARC reports from ANY domain, via the record:

*._report._dmarc.organization.com IN TXT "v=DMARC1"

dmarcian.com

Use this service for analyzing aggregate and forensic reports.

Worksheet of All Domains

Track the DMARC status of all domains in one place.

Workflow

Monitoring > Forensics > Reject

OR

Parked(Monitor) > Parked(Forensics) > Parked(Reject)

Monitoring

All domains start out by requesting aggregate reports (rua=mailto...) to check whether their mail is fully authenticated. Daily aggregate reports are sent to dmarc@organization.com and dmarcian.com.

Each domain being monitored will be given the following record:

_dmarc.example.com IN CNAME _dmarc.monitor.organization.com.

The centralized DMARC policy for monitored domains is:

_dmarc.monitor.organization.com IN TXT "v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc@organization.com,mailto:74rj5upn@ag.dmarcian.com"

Forensics

Once a domain gets to 98% pass rate according to dmarcian.com, use forensic reports (ruf=mailto:...) to clean up any remaining, unknown mail sources.

Each forensic domain will be given the following DNS records:

_dmarc.example.com IN CNAME _dmarc.forensic.organization.com

The centralized DMARC policy for forensic domains is:

_dmarc.forensic.organization.com IN TXT "v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc@organization.com,mailto:74rj5upn@ag.dmarcian.com; ruf=mailto:dmarc+forensic@organization.com,mailto:74rj5upn@fr.dmarcian.com"

Reject

After forensics are concluded, we can be certain that a domain's mail is fully authenticated. At this point, change the domain's DMARC policy to p=reject.

The centralized DMARC policy for fully authenticated domains is:

_dmarc.reject.organization.com IN TXT "v=DMARC1; p=reject; rua=mailto:dmarc@organization.com,mailto:74rj5upn@ag.dmarcian.com; ruf=mailto:dmarc+forensic@organization.com,mailto:74rj5upn@fr.dmarcian.com"

Each fully authenticated domain will be given the following DNS records:

_dmarc.example.com IN CNAME _dmarc.reject.organization.com

Parked

For security, domains that are never used for sending mail should be "parked"; all mail sent using these domains will be rejected by policy.

We'll be using the subdomain _dmarc.parked.organization.com just for human readability, so that curious folks will know by sight that the domain shouldn't be used for mail. However, it's functionally equivalent to _dmarc.reject.organization.com, so we'll just use CNAME to direct to that policy:

_dmarc.parked.organization.com IN CNAME _dmarc.reject.organization.com

Each parked domain will be given the following DNS records:

_dmarc.example.com IN CNAME _dmarc.parked.organization.com

example.com IN TXT "v=spf1 -all"

*.example.com IN TXT "v=spf1 -all"
